The "Why": Melbourne's Economic Context

To understand the proposed budget, it's crucial to compare it to local wage standards. When viewed as an "equivalent hourly wage" for a 40-hour work week, the stipend is significantly below Melbourne's legal minimum and average earnings.

This stark comparison shows the proposed $1,700/month stipend, equivalent to just $10.63/hour, is only 64% of Melbourne's legal minimum wage and less than 40% of the average wage. This isn't a request for luxury; it's a request for a foundational budget that is still deeply conservative for this city.
